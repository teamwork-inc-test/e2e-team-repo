# Customer Discovery: Decision Fatigue in Remote Teams

**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Interviewer:** Tina Teammate (Head of Product)
**Interviewees:** 5 remote team leads (Series A-B startups, 15-40 people)

## Key Findings

All five leads described the same pattern: decisions made in Slack channels get relitigated because nobody can find the original discussion.

Tina: "Every single one of them said some version of the same thing — they make a decision in a thread, three weeks later someone asks the same question, and nobody can find the thread where they decided."

### Quotes

Lead 1 (fintech, 22 people): "We tried Notion for decision logs. Nobody updates it. The decision happens in Slack and the Notion page stays empty."

Lead 3 (healthtech, 38 people): "I spend probably 4 hours a week in meetings that exist only because someone could not find a previous decision. That is $12,000 a month in wasted engineering time."

Lead 5 (devtools, 15 people): "The worst part is when you DO find the old decision but the context is gone. You see the conclusion but not the reasoning, so you end up re-debating it anyway."

### Patterns

- **Decision discovery**: 100% said finding past decisions is their #1 collaboration pain
- **Context loss**: 4/5 said even when decisions are documented, the reasoning behind them is lost
- **Tool fatigue**: 3/5 have tried and abandoned a decision-tracking tool (Notion, Confluence, custom wikis)
- **Meeting tax**: Average 3-5 hours/week in meetings that exist solely to re-establish previously made decisions

Tina: "This validates our anti-Slack thesis. The problem is not that teams cannot make decisions — they make them constantly. The problem is that decisions evaporate. Our capture mechanism needs to be zero-effort at the point of decision, not a separate documentation step."
